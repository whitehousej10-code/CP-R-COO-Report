Recruiting World-Class Coaches
Every referral in this report is only as good as the coach who services it. FY26/27 is about building the bench — and these are the ideas I brought back from the TA Disruptors Summit on how we find and select them.

What I learned at the summit
Signal quality, not volume
Pattern #1
The biggest destroyer of trust in hiring is candidates who interview well but can’t perform. More applications doesn’t mean more signal — it means more noise to filter. We recruit coaches on demonstrated ability, not polish.
The Triangle of Trust
Efficiency · Signal · Equity
A hiring process holds when three sides hold: does it sift efficiently, does it surface real ability, and does it give everyone a fair shot. We design coach hiring around all three.
Assess ability to learn
Built for 2030
With 39% of skill sets outdated by 2030, the best predictor of a world-class coach isn’t what they know today — it’s how fast they adapt. We weight learning agility above static experience.
What good looks like
• Athletes are excited to train, show up consistently and refer their friends.
• Clubhouse comms are warm, personal and frequent — the athlete never feels forgotten between sessions.
• ACR conversations are confident, outcome-led and convert because the value is genuinely clear.
• Session notes filed same day, 4 pillars completed in session, assessments booked ahead.
Where this thinking came from
The thinking on this page comes from the TA Disruptors Summit 2026 and Arctic Shores’ State of Volume Hiring 2026 research. The headline lesson for CP+R: as AI floods hiring with noise, the edge goes to teams that select on real, demonstrated ability and the capacity to learn — exactly what separates a good coach from a world-class one.
World-class coaching is what turns a referral into a retained athlete and the next referral. In FY26/27 we recruit for adaptability over static skill, select on real signal rather than interview polish, and hold every hire to the standard above. The bench we build this year is the income we protect next year.
