Annual Referral Volume
Every FY has delivered growth. The FY25/26 was the strongest FY on record, with 19 referrals on average per week. Off target by 1.9 per week.
For most the FY, we were behind FY 24/25. In the last 11 weeks I've prioritised partnerships and new relationships, helping us to exceed last year by +16 referrals.
In week 28 we set a CP+R record of 39 referrals.
When I look at the totals, I think of the phone calls, the last minute meeting requsts, change of times, "constructive feedback", political management and energy applied to our Patnerships throughout the year. I also think of the increased competition we've felt this year and how we've continued to manage new challlenges.

Referral Seasonality
May, March and April are the three busiest months of the calander year consistently, a clear spring surge and busy end to the FY. This was particularly challenging, whilst developing a HR department and managing several leavers.
June is the quietest month, a strategic month to reflect and deliver appraisals.
June, August and December are good months to take annual leave. Strategically why we scheduled Ellen's sabbatical in August and normally manage December on a rota system.
