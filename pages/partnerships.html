CP+R generated £1,137,658 in new referral income this year, up 31.0% on FY24/25. Income per referral hit £1,178, a 25.3% improvement driven by stronger athlete quality and improved EM conversion.
A pillar grew 53.9% to £800,555. My relationship with each nurse at each hospital is second-to-none. This a real strategic advantage for CP+R, keeping competition out and maturing the quality of the pathways.
B pillar declined 27.5% to £176,166. An honest reflection that I didn't deepen the relationships of each individual consultant well this year. Some of that was intentional, focusing my time on more cost effective ROI from the A-pillar and having less people to look after. Each consultant wants their own special pathway which is associated with a business proposal. Spending time with consultants is expensive, as usually they want wining and dining, which CP+R hasn't had the budget to do. There have also been missed opportunities, to capatlise on new referral sources with our newest cardiac and EM team looking after their referrals and them not converting. It's a virtuous circle
C pillar more than doubled to £160,938. More clients are finding us than ever, through our 'self-referral pathway.' Reflecting a different generation of proactive patients.

London Bridge refer to multiple CR providers and the CNS is off-site. Yet, I am still the no1 trusted person with them and cannot become complacenet. The Harbourne Birmingham and more likely the Wilmslow, Manchester would be good additions.
