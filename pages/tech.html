Q4 Executive Summary

The Hub stayed stable across Q4 with no significant downtime — the only notable interruption (a briefly inaccessible education section on 26 May, caused by a credential rotation on a dependent platform) was resolved the same day. 11 Hub tech tickets were handled, alongside recurring data & admin requests that could be reduced through clearer processes and self-service access.

The Payment System was the main focus, generating 55 tickets — roughly a 66% increase on the previous quarter — reflecting ongoing operational friction and heavy manual support around plan changes, invoice corrections and reconciliation with Xero.

New approval workflows and payment automation were introduced to cut manual developer intervention: an auditable approval modification workflow, invoice merging, earlier-than-contracted membership end dates, voiding of sent invoices, and email error alerts that improve issue visibility. The Hub also gained Superleague scoring updates and an immediate-logout security check on deactivation.

The Clubhouse had no live development, but intermittent ACR athlete login issues — most visible at contract signing during initial assessment — continue to be monitored, with diagnostic evidence gathered whenever the (now infrequent) issue recurs.

Q1 2026/27 priority: reduce Payment System workload as the new workflows take effect, identify further automations that free up team time, and where possible reinvest that time into athlete experiences — alongside a longer-term review of whether the Payment System is fit for purpose.
