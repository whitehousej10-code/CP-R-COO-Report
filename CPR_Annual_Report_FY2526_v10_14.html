About This Report

This annual performance report covers FY 2025/26 (June 2025 – May 2026) at CP+R (Clinical Prevention + Rehabilitation), a private cardiac rehabilitation provider based in London.

Prepared by Jamie Whitehouse, COO & Head of Partnerships, this report covers all business areas under Jamie's remit: external referral partnerships, onboarding, sales performance, clinical operations, NACR accreditation, assessment centres, HR and technology.

Key terms explained: NACR = National Audit of Cardiac Rehabilitation · BHC = Better Health Consultation (first clinical call with a prospective athlete) · IA = Initial Assessment (first in-person assessment at the centre) · ACR = Assessment Conversion Rate · EM = Experience Manager (sales/relationship role) · CC = Cardiac Coach · Pillar A/B/C = referral source tiers (hospitals / consultants / other channels).

This document is confidential and proprietary to Clinical Prevention + Rehabilitation Ltd.

Leadership · 06
Management and Leadership
CP+R grew its leadership depth significantly this year. Managing 26 people across clinical, commercial, operational and creative functions generated the direct insight that shaped the HR function and how the next generation of managers have been supported.
Management recommendations for FY26/27
Invest in managers before you need to
James Porter, Lewis Weekes and the pod leads are now in management roles. Structured coaching for all three should begin in Q1 FY26/27 before pressure builds.
Build HR into the rhythm of the business
Regular PDPs, structured check-ins and a clear retention programme for employees under one year of tenure should be non-negotiable calendar items, not responses to problems.
Recognition is a commercial decision
The people whose contribution is structural rather than visible deserve the most deliberate recognition. Ellen Dawson-Brown, Daniella Springett and David Jones have each been exceptional this year. Their work does not announce itself — it simply makes everything else possible. That is worth naming clearly and often.
Document institutional knowledge now
Seven forecasted departures by July 2026 represent a knowledge transfer risk. Matt Talbot's Bath site knowledge and David's system architecture need documenting before they leave.
Retention is cheaper than recruitment
43% of the team are under one year of tenure. A structured 90-day and 6-month check-in process owned by Ellie Darwin should be standard by June 2026.
Predictability reduces pressure
You know when someone has gone too long without a break. You know who will not ask for help. You know who needs recognition before they disengage. Building this awareness into management practice separates good managers from reactive ones.
