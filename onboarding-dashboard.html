RA compliance measures whether athletes are onboarded with a Recommended Activity plan in place. At 67.3% vs 90%, this was the most significant operational gap of FY25/26. High coach transition volume was a structural contributor. James Porter has named RA compliance as a stated priority for FY26/27.

The 16.5-day average from referral to BHC booking reflects strong operational throughput in a high-volume year (966 referrals). The 66.9-day end-to-end journey — referral to payment — is the key commercial metric: every day shortened here accelerates income recognition and improves athlete momentum into the programme. The 8.6-day BHC-to-IA gap is tight given assessment centre scheduling constraints. FY26/27 targets: Ref→BHC under 14 days, total journey under 60 days.
